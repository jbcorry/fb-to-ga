About Pave
What is Pave?

Pave is an marketplace lender focused on providing access to affordable credit through a personal and transparent funding experience.

Pave's 2-3 year consumer loans range in size from $3,000 up to $25,000. Pave's unique approach to underwriting takes a holistic view of a person's situation and potential, not just their FICO score

For all loans: Cross River Bank is the loan originator and Pave, Inc the loan servicer.

How is a Pave loan different from credit card debt?

Unlike credit cards' variable rates - a Pave loan has a fixed repayment term. This means on funding day 1 you'll know your exact monthly payment schedule for the term of the loan!

How is Pave able to offer low rates?

We're able to offer low rates because we personalize our approach to underwriting and take a holistic view of each borrower's unique situation.

We start by reviewing the individual's credit score and history, then incorporate additional factors like use of funds, work history, current employment, education and future earning potential. This gives us plenty of opportunities to recognize how financially responsible a person can be, and it's how we can give the lowest possible rate.

The interest rates we offer range from 5.99 - 27.1% with an origination fee of 1 - 6%.

What is Pave’s underwriting methodology?

We approach underwriting with careful consideration to an applicant's unique situation, factoring in many additional datapoints beyond the traditional credit score.

While we do use the FICO score as a baseline, we also consider alternative factors that might demonstrate financial responsibility and creditworthiness, like education, employment history, current job status and future potential.

Applying For Funds
Am I eligable for a Pave loan?

In order to be eligible for funding on Pave, you must meet a few basic requirements.

  • You must be a U.S. Citizen

  • You must be at least 18 years of age

  • You must have a U.S. bank account

  • You must have a credit score of 660 or higher. You can check your score for free here.

  • You must have an income, job offer, or you're planning on attending a course

We currently cannot offer loans to the following states:

Arizona, Connecticut, District of Columbia, Maine, Massachusetts, Nebraska, New Jersey, Nevada, North Carolina, North Dakota, Oregon, Pennsylvania, Tennessee, Vermont, West Virginia, Wisconsin and Wyoming.

Applicants under the age of 19 in Alabama and under the age of 21 in Mississippi are not eligible for funding on Pave.

How much can I borrow? And for how long?

You can borrow a minimum of $3,000 up to a maximum of $25,000 on Pave, to be repaid over 2-3 years.

Pave currently does not offer loans with longer tenures, but that's something we might expand to in the future. If you're interested in different terms, please don't hesitate to let us know!

What's the difference between an interest rate and an annual percentage rate (APR)?

An interest rate is the cost of a loan, not including fees. It is reflected as a percentage that can be used to calculate your monthly payments.

For example, let’s say you borrow $20,000 at an interest rate of 6% with a 2 year term. This means you will pay back a total of $21,200 over 2 years, broken down into monthly payments of $883.33.

An APR, or annual percentage rate, represents the cost of credit as a yearly rate. It includes not only the interest rate but also Pave’s origination fee.

The APR we offer ranges from 5.99% - 27.1%.

Can I re-apply for funds if my application is denied?

We will consider a reapplication after 30 days. This is because credit bureau changes to your profile typically take about 30 days to show up. This also applies to reapplications for different amounts.

Are there any restrictions on how I use the funds?

We know that there are lots of reasons why you might need funding, which is why we place no restrictions on how you choose to spend your loan.

Many people who use Pave spend the funding to pay for education, refinance or consolidate existing debt, start a business or even cover living expenses, for example.

Will applying for a Pave loan affect my credit?

Once you've completed the Pave questionnaire, we perform what's known as a "soft" credit check to discover your eligibility and establish a baseline for our underwriting process. Soft inquiries may be recorded in your credit report, depending on the credit bureau, but they won't affect your credit score in any way.

Please note: You have the right to obtain a copy of your credit report, free of charge, once per year from each of the three major credit reporting agencies. You can get copies from www.annualcreditreport.com.

We encourage you to do this to make sure your reports are accurate!

Does Pave accept co-signers?

Unfortunately, Pave cannot yet work with co-signers. We plan to add that as an option in the future, but it won't be for the next couple months.

If your funding needs aren't immediate, I'd recommend checking back with us then!

What if I am applying for the loan for a course, but I don't need the funds quite yet?

We want to be a timing match for when your course starts so that you're not incurring interest before necessary, and so that Pave's 3 month payment deferment option can line up with the end of your course.

We recommend applying now to find out if you qualify for a Pave loan and what your rate is! You would accept terms and complete the on-boarding process now. Thirty days before your funding date, we reassess your credit profile to make sure the lending decision and rate are still valid, and fund you closer to when you start your course.

Onboarding & Verification
What verification documents will I need to submit?

At Pave we base the lending decision on a number of factors, including your employment, your income and your assets. When filling out your application, please ensure that the income and assets you list can be supported by documentation.

We hope the following provides clear guidelines on what we can accept as verification:

Identity Verification

  • Proof of your identity (clear photo)

  • Proof of your citizenship (either a valid passport, or a combination of your driver's license + birth certificate)

Employment/Income Verification

  • Your 2 most recent pay stubs showing: your name, the pay period, your salary, your employer’s name

Asset Verification

  • Bank statements for the asset amount that you report on your application. This usually refers to bank accounts - checking, savings or investment accounts

(Unfortunately, we can’t accept mortgages, land titles or appraisals as proof of liquid assets)

If you are using your Pave loan for a course:

  • A copy of your admission letter showing your full name and start date

How does Pave verify the personal information I provide?

We manually review every loan application submitted to Pave. Once we confirm that you meet our basic requirements, we work with a credit bureau to verify your:

  • Identity (e.g. name, address, date of birth, and social security number);

  • Education (We may request official university transcipts to ensure the academic information you provide is accurate, or acceptance letters if you are intending to use your Pave loan to enroll in a course );

  • Financial history (We perform credit checks to review your current credit history and standing, and may request banking information to verify your assets)

Payments & Servicing
How long does it take to receive funds?

It should take no more than a few business days from application to funding!

  1. Apply

  2. Qualify

  3. Pave provides you with loan terms

  4. If approved, you accept the loan terms in your Pave dashboard

  5. You provide Pave identification documentation and bank account information

  6. Pave verifies your documentation and checks your bank account

  7. You hop on the phone with someone from our Pave team for a quick introduction call

  8. You receive your loan in your specified bank account

How do I make my monthly payments?

You can choose to make your monthly payments via:

  1. Automatic electronic funds transfer from your bank account

  2. Personal check by regular mail (please note that this option is subject to a $2/check processing fee)

Will I receive monthly statements by mail?

Everything is paperless - so we won't send you anything by mail, but we will send email updates when you have a monthly payment coming up.

Can I customize my payment date?

Yes, absolutely. We want the payment date to work well with your cashflow schedule. If you would like to do so, please just email our support team (hyperlink that) or give us a call.

What if I'm late in making a payment or my payment is returned?

If you fail to make your monthly payment within 15 days of its due date, we may charge a late fee (equal to the greater of 5% of past due amount or $15)

If you think you'll have trouble making an upcoming monthly payment on your Pave loan, definitely reach out and let us know.

If a payment is returned to us, we may charge a return fee of $15.

Can I defer payment?

If you are using your Pave loan to take a course, you can elect to defer the first three payments on your loan. This means you won't be required to make payments during the first three months, but interest on the loan will continue to accrue.

We will add the accrued interest amount to the unpaid principal balance of your loan after the three month period is over. Your monthly payments from that point on will be based on this combined amount.

Investing
Who is eligable to invest in Pave?

Loan investor must meet the following requirements:

  1. Be a "U.S. person" for U.S. tax purposes;

  2. Be an accredited investor; For individuals this means that (i) your income exceeds $200,000 in each of the two most recent years, or joint income with your spouse exceeds $300,000 in each of those years, and you have a reasonable expectation of the same income level in the current year, or (ii) your individual net worth, or joint net worth with your spouse, exceeds $1 million, excluding the value of your primary residence.

  3. Hold a U.S. bank account.

Due to SEC regulations, only accredited investors can invest in borrowers on Pave at this time. However, we are working hard to make Pave available to all potential investors in the near future.

Security
What is Pave's privacy policy?

We understand that your personal information is just that, personal.

Any information you share with us will always remain private and is strictly used to provide you with the best possible service.

You are in control of your account information and settings on Pave, and we promise never to share this information without your permission. Click here to read our full Privacy Policy.

If you have any questions at all, please feel free to contact us.

How does Pave keep my information safe and secure?

At Pave we understand how important it is to keep your information safe and secure. To protect your information, we follow industry-standard methods, like any financial institution, and require encryption of sensitive personal and card data stored on disk or transmitted over public networks.

Our card-processing systems adhere to PCI Data Security Standard (PCI-DSS) Level 1, and Pave's website and API are accessible via 256-bit, extended-validation SSL certificates issued by Network Solutions DV Server CA.

Pave's networks are strictly segregated according to security level with modern, restrictive firewalls protecting all connections between networks.

Customer funds are always held in a fully insured-FDIC account with our partner bank, Wells Fargo. Applications developed in-house are subject to strict quality testing and security review.
